Scan Your Paper

Make a digital copy of your handwritten template:

  • Scan at 300 DPI or higher
  • Or use a phone scanning app (Adobe Scan, Microsoft Lens)

  • Make sure it is clear and straight

Important: Write Neatly!

Your letters need to be clear and readable. The computer will turn exactly what you write into a font.

  • Do NOT use felt tip pens ‚Äî they bleed and look messy
  • Use a black fine liner pen only
  • Write each letter carefully and clearly
  • If a letter looks bad, print a new template and try again
üí°

Tips for Good Results

  • Take your time ‚Äî neat letters = nice font
  • Keep letters inside the boxes
  • Make sure each letter is easy to read
  • Wait for ink to dry before scanning
  • Write on a flat, hard surface (not on a soft notebook)

üí≠ Think About Your Font Style

Before you write, think about how you want your font to look:

  • Do you want it neat and tidy or loose and casual?
  • Will you use your normal handwriting or try something different?
  • How will this font fit with your project?
  • What mood do you want your text to have?
